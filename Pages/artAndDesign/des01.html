TRLE : Dolma's Fortress


     This is a level developed with Tomb Raider Next Generation engine; the community organize a competition called "Back to Basic", this installment of 2020, the theme was Tibet. In the competition, a team of artists provides a pack with objects, textures and background music, so the level builders make a game creating the rooms, placing the objects and making the actual gameplay.

     My level consists in a linneal experience where the player must explore a sequence of areas dealing with different challenges, though each area is a bit open to force some exploration, I didn't want to make it very broad to don't keep the player too much time in the same zones, providing new areas increased the satisfaction of progression.


     Along the way, it also includes some secret areas where the player can find resources (weapons, ammo, etc...), These secret halls, hidden by mechanisms and unusual places, aimed to be a pleasant finding, so they containt also a special treasure to increase the finding satisfaction.

     Its challenges, are not really difficult, instead, they focus in provide the solution in a way that the player learns what he is doing and why he is doing it, giving him a purpose to move along the level instead of just trying random choices.

     The level was enhaced with a subtle background plot, although there were not cinematics in the level, it manages to present the player a competitor, though other mechanisms like a short journal or seeing him running in the background. That helped to make more memorable its conflict later.

     In the player feedback, the features that liked more were:

  • The traps room became the big favourite between the players.
  • The chase scene and the final fate of the competitor become also original and praised.
  • The ambience, making unique visuals that helps to be memorable and attractive.
  • The use of the background music and cameras to guide the gameplay mood.
  • The funny gameplay, though some veterans players though it was too easy, most of the feedback reflected enjoyment for the experience.
